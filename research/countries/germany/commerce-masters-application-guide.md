**Last verified:** 2026-07-23

This guide explains **how to apply**, **eligibility**, **difficulty**, and **realistic admission chances** for each programme, assuming a typical **Indian 3-year B.Com or BBA** (English or bilingual medium, ~180 ECTS equivalent when mapped). It is not legal or immigration advice; always confirm deadlines and rules on the **official programme page** for your target intake (usually **winter semester**, start October).

---

## How applications work in Germany (India profile)

### Typical steps

1. **Check degree recognition** — Most universities expect a bachelor equivalent to **180 ECTS** (or **≥140 ECTS** at application if you graduate later). Indian 3-year degrees are often accepted if the university is rated **H+** in [ANABIN](https://anabin.kmk.org/anabin.html) and modules map to required ECTS buckets.
2. **APS certificate (India)** — Required by several universities (e.g. **TUM**, **Hohenheim IBE**, **Goethe Frankfurt MMS**, **FAU MIBS**). This is **not** the same as uni-assist. Process via the Academic Evaluation Centre for your country (often coordinated with German Embassy / APS India). Plan **several weeks to months**.
3. **uni-assist VPD** — For many universities, non-German degrees need a **Vorprüfungsdokumentation** from [uni-assist](https://www.uni-assist.de/en/how-to-apply/plan-your-application/vpd): grade conversion (often modified Bavarian formula), document check, and statement of eligibility. Apply in **My assist** for the **specific university**; processing often **4–6+ weeks**. Fee applies per VPD request.
4. **University portal** — Almost all programmes also require a **direct online application** (TUMonline, Campo, HohCampus, Mannheim portal, etc.). uni-assist alone is rarely sufficient.
5. **Enrollment & visa** — After admission: enroll, blocked account / finances, national visa, residence permit. Post-study **18-month job seeker** (Section 20 AufenthG) applies if other conditions are met.

### Common documents

- Degree certificate + transcripts (often **certified** copies for non-EU)
- CV, sometimes motivation letter or essay
- English test (see programme; **B2–C1** is typical)
- Module descriptions / syllabus for **ECTS mapping** (critical for Passau, Paderborn, Magdeburg, Würzburg, TUM)
- GMAT/GRE where listed
- APS / VPD when required

## How to read difficulty & admission chance

| **Difficulty** | Meaning |
|----------------|---------|
| **Very high** | National top tier; points/GMAT/aptitude test; few seats; strong quant or STEM expectations |
| **High** | Selective ranking, interview/video, or strict grade conversion (≤2.3 German) |
| **Medium–high** | Competitive NC or aptitude test weighted heavily |
| **Medium** | Solid grades + correct ECTS mapping; less famous brand |
| **Medium–low** | Newer or regional programme; more places relative to demand |

| **Chance (typical good BBA profile)** | Meaning |
|---------------------------------------|---------|
| **Low** | Apply only if you exceed requirements (GMAT, grade, STEM ECTS, experience abroad) |
| **Low–moderate** | Competitive; 1 strong differentiator needed |
| **Moderate** | Reasonable fit if ECTS and grades map well |
| **Moderate–good** | Good fit if documents are complete and grades convert favorably |
| **N/A** | Programme not open to new applicants |

*“Good BBA profile” here ≈ **65–75%** or **~7.0–7.8/10** Indian GPA, English **IELTS 6.5–7.0**, **no** mandatory study abroad unless stated.*

## Programme details (same order as CSV)

### 1–2. Technical University of Munich (TUM)

**Official:** [M&T how to apply](https://www.mgt.tum.de/programs/master-in-management-technology/how-to-apply) · [Consumer Science apply](https://www.mgt.tum.de/programs/master-in-consumer-science/how-to-apply) · [TUM application info](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/application/application-info-portal/online-application)

**How to apply:** Register in **TUMonline** → complete programme application → parallel **uni-assist** application for **VPD** (must be submitted before deadline; VPD document may follow later per TUM rules) → upload **APS** (India).

**Eligibility (M&T):** Bachelor ≥**140 ECTS** at apply; ≥**25 ECTS** management, **10** economics, **15** engineering/natural sciences/math/CS — **Indian BBA often fails the 15 STEM ECTS** unless electives included physics/math/CS. **Consumer Affairs:** management/econ/social science background; check FPSO for quant ECTS.

**Tests:** **GMAT Classic/Focus ≥65th percentile mandatory** for degrees from India (and China, Pakistan, Egypt, Bangladesh). English: **IELTS 6.5** or TOEFL 88, or 12 ECTS English-taught modules, or GMAT ≥53rd %ile as English proof.

**Selection:** Two-stage **aptitude assessment** (points then possible admission test). No essay/interview for M&T.

**Difficulty / chance:** M&T **very high**; **low** for standard commerce without STEM. Consumer Affairs **high**; **low–moderate** with strong GMAT and relevant thesis/internships.

### 3. LMU — M.Sc. Management & Digital Technologies (MMT)

**Official:** [Application & submission](https://www.som.lmu.de/en/studies/study-offer/master-management-digital-technologies/application-and-submission/)

**How to apply:** **Direct** online form (Feb–May) + essay; international students also follow LMU enrollment via International Office after admission.

**Eligibility:** **180 ECTS** bachelor; **business** with ≥**20 ECTS informatics** (or inverse); **15 ECTS** of minor at application; grade ≤**2.3** on German scale; **C1** English.

**Selection:** Essay review → shortlisted candidates invited to **aptitude assessment** (written/oral, often June).

**Difficulty / chance:** **Very high** — Indian BBA without CS/informatics minor needs LMU’s **Basics in Programming** route or extra modules; **low** unless GPA converts to ≤2.3 and essay/assessment excel.

### 4. University of Mannheim — Mannheim Master in Management (English track)

**Official:** [MMM application FAQ](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/programs/master/mmm/application-information/) · [Selection statute PDF](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/media/Einrichtungen/zula/Auswahlsatzungen_master/satzung_ma_mmm_en.pdf)

**How to apply:** **Direct** Mannheim portal (**not uni-assist**). Period **1 April – 15 May** (English track). Proof of English may be submitted by **15 August** if pending.

**Eligibility:** **180 ECTS** (≥**140** if still studying); ≥**36 ECTS** pure **business administration**; **C1 English** (IELTS **7.0**, TOEFL **100**, or ≥40% of bachelor taught in English).

**Tests:** **GMAT/GRE optional** but up to **60** ranking points (GMAT Focus ≥**555** or classic ≥**600** for full points). Ranking also uses grade (60 pts), semester abroad (22), econ/math/stats ECTS (32), internships (6).

**Difficulty / chance:** **Very high** — Germany’s flagship MiM; **low–moderate** without GMAT and strong grade; **moderate** with GMAT 600+ equivalent and 7.5+ GPA equivalent.

### 5. Goethe University Frankfurt — M.Sc. Management Science

**Official:** [Application](https://www.wiwi.uni-frankfurt.de/en/study/study-programs/master/master-in-management-science/application) · [Selection process](https://www.wiwi.uni-frankfurt.de/en/study/study-programs/master/master-in-management-science/selection-process)

**How to apply:** **uni-assist** online (**no uni-assist handling fee** for Frankfurt). **15 January – 15 May**; documents cannot be added after deadline.

**Eligibility:** Bachelor in business/economics or related with ≥**90 ECTS** in economics/management/BWL; **APS** for India. English **IELTS 7.0** / TOEFL 95. Apply with ≥**80%** of bachelor credits if degree pending.

**Tests:** GMAT/GRE **optional** but used in points-based selection (~**42 places**).

**Difficulty / chance:** **Very high**; cohort grades are strong — **low** for average Indian BBA unless quant-heavy transcript and optional GMAT/GRE strengthen ranking.

## Suggested application strategy (Indian B.Com / BBA)

1. **Tier your list:** 2–3 **reach** (Mannheim, Frankfurt, FAU, LMU, TUM if STEM+GMAT), 4–6 **match** (Hohenheim, Würzburg, Passau with GMAT, Paderborn, Magdeburg, TU Berlin), 2–3 **safety** (Chemnitz, Siegen, Oldenburg if languages fit).
2. **Start APS + uni-assist early** (January–February for October intake).
3. **Prepare module handbooks** in English with ECTS mapping tables for strict faculties.
4. **Take GMAT** if applying to TUM (India), Passau BA, Augsburg GBM, or Mannheim/Frankfurt for ranking points.
5. **Close MEMS** from your active list until Humboldt reopens a successor programme.
